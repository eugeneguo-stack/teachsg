# Pythagoras' Theorem & Trigonometry
[stextbox caption="Pythagoras' theorem" stextbox id="default"]$a^2+b^2=c^2$
[/stextbox]

[stextbox caption="Trigonometric ratios" stextbox id="download"]$\tan\theta=\frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}}$
$\cos\theta=\frac{\text{adjacent}}{\text{hypotenuse}}$
$\sin\theta=\frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{hypotenuse}}$

<strong>TOA CAH SOH</strong> is applicable for only right-angled triangles[/stextbox]

[stextbox caption="Obtuse angles" stextbox id="info"]$\sin(180^\circ-\theta)=\sin\theta$
$\cos(180^\circ-\theta)=-\cos\theta$
[/stextbox]

[stextbox caption="Sine rule" stextbox id="default"]$\dfrac{a}{\sin A}=\dfrac{b}{\sin B}$
[/stextbox]

[stextbox caption="Cosine rule" stextbox id="download"]$c^2=a^2+b^2-2 ab \cos C$
[/stextbox]

[stextbox caption="Area of triangle" stextbox id="info"]$\text{Area of triangle}=\frac{1}{2}ab \sin C$
[/stextbox]

[stextbox caption="Bearings" stextbox id="default"]A bearing is a <strong>3-digit positive number</strong> with units of degree to show direction <strong>clockwise</strong> from the north direction.
[/stextbox]
