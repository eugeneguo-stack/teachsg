# Data Analysis
[stextbox caption="Mode" stextbox id="default"]Mode is the <strong>most frequently occurring</strong> number. A set of data can have <strong>more than one</strong> mode.
[/stextbox]

[stextbox caption="Mean" stextbox id="download"]$\text{mean} = \dfrac{\text{sum of all numbers}}{\text{number of numbers}}$
[/stextbox]

[stextbox caption="Median" stextbox id="info"]Median is the <strong>centre</strong> number when the numbers are arranged from <strong>smallest to largest</strong>.
[/stextbox]

[stextbox caption="Range" stextbox id="default"]Range = maximum $-$ minimum
[/stextbox]

[stextbox caption="Quartiles & percentiles" stextbox id="download"]0<sup>th</sup> percentile: minimum
25<sup>th</sup> percentile: lower quartile
50<sup>th</sup> percentile: median
75<sup>th</sup> percentile: upper quartile
100<sup>th</sup> percentile: maximum
[/stextbox]

[stextbox caption="Interquartile range" stextbox id="info"]Interquartile range = upper quartile $-$ lower quartile
[/stextbox]

[stextbox caption="Mean & standard deviation" stextbox id="default"]<strong>Ungrouped</strong>
$\text{Mean}, \bar{x} =\frac{\sum x}{N}$
$\text{Standard deviation}, \sigma =\sqrt{\frac{\sum(x-\overline{x})^2}{N}}$

<strong>Grouped</strong>
$\text{Mean}, \overline{x}=\frac{\sum fx}{\sum f}$
$\text{Standard deviation}, \sigma =\sqrt{\frac{\sum fx^2}{\sum f}-\left(\frac{\sum fx}{\sum f}\right)^2}$
[/stextbox]
